Methodology and context

Built as a visual explanation of age-based voting dynamics.

This site combines publicly reported turnout patterns with explanatory framing designed for fast understanding, exploration, and classroom-style discussion.

How to read this site

Interpretation framework

1. Turnout first

Participation differences by age often explain more electoral outcomes than preference shifts alone.

2. Cohorts evolve

Generations do not stay fixed; their priorities and voting habits change across life stages.

3. Context matters

High-salience cycles can temporarily compress turnout gaps before structural patterns re-emerge.

4. Signals over absolutes

The goal is trend interpretation and directional insight, not deterministic forecasting.
