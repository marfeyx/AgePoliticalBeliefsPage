Generation-level breakdown

The age divide is structural, not temporary.

Different age groups are shaped by distinct media ecosystems, policy priorities, and life-stage pressures—so they show up and vote differently.

Registration Friction

First-time voters face paperwork and timing barriers that older habitual voters already solved years ago.

Strongest impact: under 30

Issue Compression

Younger voters prioritize cost-of-living and climate urgency, while older voters emphasize stability and institutional continuity.

Cross-generational tension

Campaign Attention

Parties target high-propensity voters first, reinforcing turnout advantages among older cohorts.

Feedback loop effect

Local Embeddedness

Long-term residence and deeper community ties increase awareness of elections and local stakes.

Highest in 45+

Digital Mobilization

Rapid-response organizing on social platforms can create sudden spikes in youth turnout during high-salience cycles.

Fast but volatile

Trust & Efficacy

People vote more when they believe institutions still respond to civic participation.

Core turnout driver
